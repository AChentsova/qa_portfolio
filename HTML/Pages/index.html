Stray Kids

Stray Kids (Korean: 스트레이 키즈) is a South Korean boy band formed by JYP Entertainment through the 2017 reality show of the same name.

The group is composed of eight members: Bang Chan, Lee Know, Changbin, Hyunjin, Han, Felix, Seungmin, and I.N.

Name

The group's name "Stray Kids" was not defined by JYP Entertainment but conceptualized by the members themselves.
It originally referred to a lost child who wants to chase their dreams and later evolved to represent the idea of finding a way together out of the ordinary.

History

  1. 2017–2018: Formation, debut and I Am series
  2. 2019: Clé series and Woojin's departure
  3. 2020: Japanese debut, 生 series and All In
  4. 2021: Kingdom: Legendary War and Noeasy
  5. 2022: Oddinary, Circus and Maxident
  6. 2023–present: The Sound, 5-Star, Social Path / Super Bowl (Japanese Ver.) and Rock-Star

Artistry

Stray Kids' music is generally K-pop, hip hop, and electronic.
They are considered a "self-producing" idol group as the members are almost always involved in songwriting and composing, and sometimes assist in arranging.
Since the release of "God's Menu" in 2020, Stray Kids' discography has also been described as being of a unique "mala taste genre" and "noise music", a label that inspired their album Noeasy.

Members

On October 28, 2019, Woojin left Stray Kids and terminated his contract with JYP Entertainment for undisclosed personal reasons.
From February to July 2021, Hyunjin was on hiatus due to bullying allegations against him but resumed regular activities with the group in August
